## Learning Approach

The documentation is organized to help you learn robotics concepts step by step before attempting the tasks.

Each task follows this learning flow:

1. **Learn the Concept**
   - Go through the learning material and tutorials.
   - Understand the robotics concepts used in the task.

2. **Practice**
   - Explore the provided Webots world.
   - Experiment with the starter controller.

3. **Implement**
   - Develop your own solution by completing the controller.
   - Test your implementation thoroughly.

4. **Submit**
   - Verify your solution.
   - Record the required demonstration.
   - Submit the requested files.

> **Tip:** Complete the learning material before attempting the task. The concepts build on one another throughout the course.

---

## Documentation Structure

The documentation is divided into the following sections:

- **Getting Started** – Installation and project setup.
- **Live Sessions** – Session recordings and additional explanations.
- **Tasks** – Problem statements, resources, and submission instructions.
- **Resources** – Useful references and external links.

---

## Development Guidelines

While implementing your solution:

- Write clean and readable code.
- Use meaningful variable and function names.
- Add comments wherever necessary.
- Avoid hardcoding values whenever possible.
- Test your controller after every major change.

---

## Submission Guidelines

Before submitting your work, ensure that:

- Your controller runs without errors.
- The required functionality is implemented correctly.
- The demonstration video clearly shows the task execution.
- All required files are included.

---

## Good Practices

- Test your solution multiple times.
- Debug one module at a time.
- Read console messages carefully.
- Keep a backup of your working controller.
- Ask questions whenever you are stuck.

---

## Academic Integrity

Your submission should represent your own work.

- Do not copy solutions from others.
- Do not share your controller files publicly.
- Understand every line of code you submit.
- External resources may be used only for learning and reference.

---

## Need Help?

If you encounter any issues:

- Review the learning material.
- Check the FAQs and troubleshooting guides.
- Attend the live sessions.
- Contact the mentors through the official communication channels.
